# Meta-Evolution of Features — When Evolution Begins to Redesign Its Own Representation

## Overview
Classical machine learning fixes its representation:
- fixed features  
- fixed embeddings  
- fixed normalization  
- fixed architecture  
- fixed perceptual frame  

All learning happens *within* that representation.

But evolved intelligence does something much deeper:

**It evolves the representation itself.**

This is the moment evolution becomes self-referential —  
when the system begins to evolve *its own perceptual substrate*.

This is the forty-third pillar of evolved generalization.

---

## The Core Insight
> **True intelligence emerges not when a system adapts within a representation,  
> but when it adapts the representation itself.**

This is the beginning of self-design.

This is the boundary where learning transforms into **meta-learning** —  
but not gradient-based meta-learning, which is optimization of optimization.

This is **evolution of representation**.

---

## Why Fixed Representations Limit ML
Every ML model encodes:
- one embedding basis  
- one geometry  
- one feature map  
- one normalization  
- one structural bias  

This leads to:
- overfitting to the chosen representation  
- massive brittleness under drift  
- hallucination when representation becomes mismatched  
- inability to adapt to new domains  
- permanent dependence on retraining  
- catastrophic forgetting  

The representation becomes a cage.

---

## How Evolution Breaks the Cage
Evolution allows:
- new features to emerge  
- old features to die  
- new perceptual biases to evolve  
- scaling and normalization to adapt  
- binning strategies to shift  
- resolution to grow or shrink  
- perspectives to be reweighted  
- sensory channels to be added or removed  

This is meta-evolution.

Evolution is not optimizing *within* the representation.  
Evolution is optimizing *the representation itself*.

---

## L7A as an Early Meta-Evolution System
L7A demonstrates this through:

### 1. **Trace deletion**
Evolution can “zero out” a trace, effectively removing a perceptual dimension.

### 2. **Evolving within-bin structures**
Bins stabilize or collapse depending on usefulness.

### 3. **Scale shifts**
Narrow-to-broad scale evolution changes how the map perceives behavior.

### 4. **Soft-cell adjustments**
The soft-cell stage is an evolved pre-processor — not designed, but selected.

### 5. **Resolution adaptation**
Bin width evolves according to the signal-to-noise structure.

### 6. **Population-selection of alternative views**
Different agents encode different representations; selection chooses the best.

L7A is not just learning patterns —  
it is evolving *ways of seeing*.

## Meta-Evolution and Drift Survival
The world changes:
- regimes shift

A fixed representation collapses.

A meta-evolving representation adapts.
