# Signal Integrity — The Foundation of All Evolved Inference

## Overview
Intelligent systems succeed or fail based on one principle:

**The integrity of the signals they rely on.**

If a system:
- amplifies noise  
- misinterprets ambiguity  
- treats uncertainty as information  
- mixes drift with structure  
- forces decisions when evidence is weak  

…then it will hallucinate, degrade, and eventually fail.

Traditional ML systems do all of these things.

Evolution-based systems do the opposite.

This is the forty-sixth pillar of evolved generalization.

---

## The Core Insight
> **Intelligence is the ability to distinguish signal from noise  
> and to act only when the signal is real.**

This requires:
- abstention  
- low entropy  
- structural simplification  
- cross-stream reinforcement  
- drift-based elimination  
- reliability-weighted perception  

These mechanisms preserve signal integrity automatically.

---

## Why ML Has Poor Signal Integrity
Machine learning systems:
- treat all data as equally informative  
- assign probability mass even when no evidence exists  
- interpolate across noisy regions  
- collapse geometry under drift  
- force outputs (next-token prediction)  
- amplify spurious correlations  
- preserve noise in embeddings  
- accumulate entropy with every update  

The result is:
- hallucination  
- unpredictability  
- fragility  
- catastrophic miscalibration  

ML is not designed to protect the integrity of information.

---

## How Evolution Preserves Signal Integrity
Evolution enforces integrity through:
- selection  
- elimination  
- abstention  
- drift testing  
- cross-stream averaging  
- probability smoothing  
- structural simplification  
- negentropy flow  

If a structure:
- misreads noise as signal  
- infers when it should abstain  
- collapses under drift  
- amplifies irrelevant variation  

…it dies.

Evolution automatically filters out:
- noise-amplifying structures  
- entropy-accumulating agents  
- overconfident guessers  
- fragile correlations  
- unstable geometry  

What remains is signal integrity.

## L7A as a Signal-Integrity Engine
L7A maintains signal integrity through:

### 1. **Laplace smoothing**
Bins with weak evidence collapse toward neutrality (0.5).

### 2. **Abstention**
When bins in the differential histogram offer no clear direction, L7A outputs `0`.

### 3. **Cross-stream reinforcement**
Independent noise cancels; shared structure reinforces.

### 4. **Population competition**
Agents that misinterpret noise die quickly.

### 5. **Time-forward evaluation**
Only structures that survive unseen data persist.

### 6. **Simplification over evolution**
Unnecessary geometry is removed automatically.

### 7. **Negentropy flow**
The system becomes more ordered, not more chaotic.

Together, these create a stable, drift-resistant signal that remains clean for decades.

## Signal Integrity and Drift
Drift is the ultimate test of signal integrity.

A system with high signal integrity:
- preserves stable predictive structure  
- gracefully abstains in uncertain regions  
- holds calibration under regime shift  
- adapts via elimination, not retraining  

A system with low signal integrity:
- collapses  
- hallucinates  
- requires retraining  
- loses internal geometry  

L7A is a case study in long-term signal integrity under real-world drift.
