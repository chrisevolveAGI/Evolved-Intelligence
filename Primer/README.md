AGI PRIMER

A Complete Conceptual Framework for Intelligence in a Drifting World

This Primer introduces the foundational principles that distinguish true
intelligence from interpolation, memorization, and static optimization. It
explains why modern AI systems—despite their scale and sophistication—lack
the evolutionary stability required for generalization, and it outlines the
architecture necessary for constructing intelligence that survives drift.

At its core, the Primer asserts a single law:

Intelligence is the ability to preserve stable structure under drift.
Any system that fails this test is not intelligent, no matter how large,
accurate, or expressive.

Evolution, continuous out-of-sample validation, and abstention form the
three pillars of drift-resistant intelligence. This Primer provides the
conceptual foundation for building systems that adhere to these principles.
