# 1. The Problem: Single-Channel Systems Inherit Single-Channel Noise

A single-stream model (e.g., only the S&P 500) inherits:

- its shock profile  
- its idiosyncratic noise  
- its temporary distortions  
- its microstructure artifacts  
- its regime-specific quirks  

This means:

- noise passes directly into the inference engine  
- fragile structures can appear stable  
- drift can masquerade as true signal  

Multi-channel systems solve this.

- its own idiosyncratic events  
- its own microstructure noise  

These noise streams are **not perfectly correlated**.
